Outcome Tracking System

Quality management patient survey allows Medbelle measure customer satisfaction and quantify all their hard work while improving their processes based on customer’s feedback.

My Role

In this project I was Product Designer, UX Researcher and Visual Designer. The aim was to design mobile-first Outcome Tracking System Application.

The Challenge

No one likes filling out surveys, especially when there’s no incentive for the participant. Surveys, forms and other forms of market research are perceived by users tedious and often unpleasant.

This is especially true when it comes to market research tools and survey designs. The real challenge was bringing to the table design that's simple yet beautiful, nudging users towards full survey completion.

The Design

I knew that design needs to be clean and simple for easy readability on primarily mobile devices. I used icons to mark responses for the questionnaires with likert-scale questions to capture the attention of the audience. Progress bar shows users how far they are and how many more questions are awaiting them contributing to the average completion rate as many people abandon filling out surveys when not knowing how long it will take.

Working Towards Business Goals

The goal of quality management tool is to successfully collect the data. I wanted to make it simple and timeless so the created design could serve for years to come with minimum maintenance. I also took in the consideration the fact that before any market research participants always have the questions that are covered by following categories:

  • How long will this take? - making sure to inform the audience how long will the survey will take so they can set their expectations and not abandon it halfway.
  • Why are you asking me this? - communicate to your audience why this is important. Users need to know why they should compromise their time to your cause.
  • What am I getting out of this? - what is the incentive for taking part in this?

The survey design considered all the above questions. This strategic thinking helped set right expectations for the users and gave them an accurate sense of the whole survey process.

Mobile-friendly

Most company’s website traffic comes from mobile devices. It was crucial to design a product that would look good on smaller mobile screens and is easily tappable.

Project Outcomes

With my wireframes engineering was able to easily develop the missing feature and ship the item very quickly. This project saved a lot of time, nerves and resources for the development team. Now the Medbelle team has tool aiding them to quantitatively measure customer satisfaction and they are using these numbers to improve their services.
